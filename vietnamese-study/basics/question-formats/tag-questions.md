# Tag questions

Vietnamese has numerous question particles. They are rarely (if ever) used in combination; so each of these below should only be used by themselves and never paired with another particle.&#x20;

### Không

_Không_ is literally _no_ and can be applied at the end of practically any regular statement to convert it into a question. Sometimes _có (have)_ and _phải (right, correct, rightly)_ are also used for additional grammatical function.

subject verb no ?   —   bạn ăn không?   —   do you eat?&#x20;

subject verb right no ?   —   bạn ăn phải không?   —   must you eat?&#x20;

subject have verb no ?   —   bạn có ăn không?   —   have you eaten? (a bit like past perfect i think)

subject have want verb right no ?   —   bạn có muốn ăn phải không?   —   do you want to eat?&#x20;

### Chưa

_Chưa_ is directly _yet_, and also _not yet_ when used in statement form. (Actually, if you think about it, _yet_ and _not yet_ have exactly the same meaning; we just include the negation word _not_ according to our grammar.) Use _chưa_ at the end of a sentence **instead of** _không_ or other question particle.

subject verb yet ?   —   bạn ăn chưa?   —   did you eat yet?&#x20;

### Chứ

subject verb right(im right) ?

### Ạ

subject verb \[polite] (?)&#x20;

### Hả

subject verb, huh(mild anger) ?&#x20;

### Rồi

subject verb already ? — question implied via context and intonation, e.g. a falling tone will fall less&#x20;
