## What are sonorants?

[_Sonorants_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonorant) are one of two primary superclasses of sounds produced in speech.

The other is [_Obstruents_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obstruent) — [plosives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plosive) (p, b, t, d, k, g, etc), [fricatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fricative) (f, v, θ, ð, s, z, ʃ, ʒ, etc), [taps & flaps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tap_and_flap_consonants) (), and others.

Sonorants include:

As you can see, there are a lot of available phones to choose from! And this is not an exhaustive list. So by no means would it be a challenging language to create.

It would most likely feature a lot of diphthongs, and probably some pronunciation­—grammar morphology by which certain sounds are metastasised in order to produce word mutations, due to the smaller range of available consonants.
